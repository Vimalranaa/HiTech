    • Fire hoses are fundamental tools in firefighting operations, allowing firefighters to deliver a powerful stream of water or fire-suppressing agents to extinguish fires and protect lives and property. They are often used in conjunction with other firefighting equipment, such as fire hydrants, pumps, and nozzles, to create an effective firefighting system.

    The fire reel drum incorporates a mechanism that allows for the controlled winding and unwinding of the fire hose. This mechanism can be manual or automatic, depending on the specific design. Manual reels require the user to rotate the drum to wind or unwind the hose, while automatic reels have a mechanism that uses a spring or motor for automated hose retrieval.

    The fire reel drum can accommodate a specific length of fire hose, which can vary depending on the design and intended application. Common lengths range from 20 meters (65 feet) to 36 meters (118 feet) or more. The capacity of the drum determines the amount of hose that can be stored and deployed.
